For Calum

On Sunday 25th September 2016, I broke my face.

Playing 11-aside football, I jumped up for a 50/50 challenge to head the ball and then BOOM. Or crack, rather.

Clinically speaking, I had an “open internal reduction of the right zygomatic arch”. Meaning: my cheekbone was in pieces and my jaw and eye socket were also cracked. I literally had a broken face.

The following pages are some random thoughts I had during my recovery. This book was made to mark the end of the healing process. A cathartic reflection of my facial trauma.

It was also made as a thank you to the people in my life who took such good care of me during a time I couldn't do the two things I love most: eat and play football.

I wish the A&E staff told me not to blow my nose.
Because when I did all my bones moved.
At least now I know it's broken.

Somebody needs to put art on the ceilings of hospitals.

True love is a boyfriend that buys a food processor.

Please don't ask me if it was painful.
I broke my face.
Of course it was painful.

Today I walked 76 steps. It all counts.

Affogato: breakfast of champions. One part coffee, two parts ice cream.

Yawned for the first time today since the accident.

Did you know that you can buy codeine over-the-counter?

Top tip: baked camembert with a spoon is an excellent meal. So soft.

There should be labels on hospital gowns so you know which way to wear them. Apparently mine is on backwards.

Titanium has a 3000 degree Fahrenheit melting point and a hexagonal closed packed atomic structure. It's also corrosion resistant and has a ridiculously high tensile strength. Oh, and it doesn't set off metal detectors.

Eye tests shouldn't spell real words. It's confusing. Do you read the letters or the words?

Nurses have to put up with so much shit. And vomit (Sorry).

Why are there so many pistachios in bircher muesli?

Thanksgiving. Today I'm thankful that I've never experienced domestic violence.
Because I've seen the way people look at me in public like I'm a victim. I'm only a victim of my own competitiveness. It must be hard for sufferers to hide their pain, repeatedly when everyone just stares at you.

My surgeon gets me to send him selfies (“taken in good light”) to monitor my progress. The NHS is amazing.

First solid meal today. Pizza.
It took 2 hours to eat and it was blissful.

In my job contract it says that sick leave is covered in the event of an accident or injury except those “sustained whilst playing a dangerous sport”. Is football a dangerous sport?

It's a terrible idea to try and take out your own stitches with toe nail clippers. Let them dissolve on their own or leave it to the professionals.

Today I walked past the scene of the accident. The physical scars have healed. Emotional ones have not.

Opiate withdrawal can manifest itself in a lot of ways. For example a face rash.

12 more days until medical clearance.
6 weeks have now passed. Can I be better now?

Everyday I stare into the mirror and stroke my face. Even though I can't feel it, I just want to make sure it's still there.
